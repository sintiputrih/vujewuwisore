The top 10 Backup Quarterbacks of All Time

Publish date: 2024-05-23

Backup quarterbacks play a crucial role in the NFL, often stepping in to lead their teams to victory in the absence of the starting quarterback. While they may not always receive the spotlight, their contributions are invaluable. Here, we'll delve into the top 10 backup quarterbacks of all time based on their impact and achievements in the league.

1. Earl Morrall

Earl Morrall stands out as one of the greatest backup quarterbacks in NFL history. As a journeyman quarterback, Morrall's defining moments include filling in for Johnny Unitas with the Colts and later backing up Bob Griese for the Miami Dolphins. His remarkable 1972 season, where he went 11-0 as a starter to preserve the Dolphins' perfect 17-0 season, solidifies his place among the best backup quarterbacks.

2. Kerry Collins

Kerry Collins' longevity and ability to step up when his team needed him make him a standout backup quarterback. Throughout his 16-season career, Collins made significant contributions as a backup, notably leading Washington to the playoffs in 2007 with a 3-0 record as a replacement for Jason Campbell.

3. Brian Hoyer

Brian Hoyer's resilience and consistency in the league have earned him a spot among the top backup quarterbacks. With 13 years in the NFL, he has started 39 games and provided veteran insurance for various teams, showcasing the value of an experienced backup quarterback.

4. Jordan Love

While still early in his career, Jordan Love's potential as a backup quarterback cannot be overlooked. As the potential successor to Aaron Rodgers, Love's performance and readiness to step into a starting role will be closely monitored. His trajectory in the league has the potential to cement his status among the greatest backup quarterbacks.

5. Kyle Orton

Kyle Orton, known for his contributions as a backup quarterback for the Dallas Cowboys, has been a topic of discussion due to his valuable services and role in the team. Despite not cracking the top 10 list, Orton's impact as a backup quarterback is noteworthy.

6. Wade Wilson

Wade Wilson's dual role as a player and quarterbacks coach for the Cowboys showcases his influence both on and off the field. His longevity in the league and contributions as a backup quarterback and coach position him as one of the top backup quarterbacks in Cowboys history.

7. Matt Flynn

Matt Flynn's success as a backup quarterback, particularly during his relief appearances for Aaron Rodgers, highlights the significance of a reliable backup in a team's roster. His achievements, including securing a lucrative contract with the Seahawks, underscore his impact as a backup quarterback.

8. Trent Dilfer

Trent Dilfer's career as a backup quarterback and his pivotal role in leading the Baltimore Ravens to a Super Bowl victory in 2001 solidify his position among the top backup quarterbacks. His ability to step up in crucial moments exemplifies the importance of a dependable backup quarterback.

9. Doug Pederson

Doug Pederson's transition from a backup quarterback to a successful head coach adds to his legacy in the NFL. His contributions as a backup, including leading the Eagles to the playoffs in 1999, showcase his impact and resilience in the league.

10. Nick Foles

Nick Foles' remarkable performance as a backup quarterback for the Philadelphia Eagles, culminating in a Super Bowl MVP title, cements his place among the top backup quarterbacks of all time. His ability to excel in high-pressure situations underscores the value of a reliable backup quarterback in the NFL.

The role of a backup quarterback is often overlooked, but their impact on the success of a team cannot be understated. The top 10 backup quarterbacks of all time have demonstrated resilience, skill, and the ability to lead their teams to victory when called upon. Their contributions have left an indelible mark on the NFL, solidifying their places among the league's greatest backup quarterbacks.
