Early Life and Career

Dell Curry was born in Harrisonburg, Virginia, and attended Fort Defiance High School. He later attended Virginia Tech, where he played college basketball. His professional career began when he was selected by the Utah Jazz in the 1986 NBA Draft. He played for several teams, including the Cleveland Cavaliers, Milwaukee Bucks, and Toronto Raptors, but his most notable stint was with the Charlotte Hornets.

Professional Basketball Career

Curry’s professional basketball career spanned 16 seasons, during which he established himself as one of the best shooters in the NBA. His career earnings from his time as a player are estimated to be around $20 million. His highest single-season salary was $2.4 million during the 1997-1998 season with the Charlotte Hornets.

Post-Basketball Career

After retiring from professional basketball, Curry transitioned into sports commentary. He has been a color commentator for the Charlotte Hornets television broadcasts since 2009. This role has significantly contributed to his net worth.
