Who Was B Smyth? Bio, Parents, Girlfriend, Death Details, Height

Publish date: 2024-08-03

R&B singer, B Smyth passed away in November 2022, 3 weeks after releasing a single from his ICU bed. Fortunately, “he was able to have a smoother process” from all the love and light fans were sending on social media.

This B Smyth Bio explores his life.

B Smyth Death Details

B Smyth, whose real name was Brandon Smith, took his last breath on November 17, 2022. He passed away on a Thursday morning from respiratory failure after a long battle with pulmonary fibrosis.

“On behalf of my brother and my family we want to say thank you to all of you for all of your love & support throughout the years,” Smyth’s brother Denzi broke the tragic news on his IG. Also, Denzi asked for privacy during these difficult times and thanked everyone for all of their prayers. 

It seems Smyth’s final days were well spent. He was happily watching the dance challenges for his latest released single “Twerkaholic, Pt.2” over TikTok and IG. “He was able to have a smoother process… All the love and light you were sending on social media he really felt it and sunk into it,” Denzi explained.

Furthermore, Denzi said that his brother asked him to make the video to let his fans know he “appreciates all the things you guys do for him,” and said that it was his fans that inspired him on his new projects with music or fashion.

Did B Smyth Leave Behind A Girlfriend Or A Wife?

B Smyth surely didn’t have a wife. But he was looking for a girlfriend earlier the year he passed.

“Looking for a Valentine. Is that you?” he made a TikTok video in February 2022.

Thereafter, the singer stopped talking about his dating life. So, we don’t know if Smyth found the love he was looking for or not.

But with that being said, there was no shortage of admirers who were head over heels for the singer.

“I should go on catfish & say I’m dating @TheRealBSmyth lmao I wish. with yo s*xy a*s,” a fan wrote.

While another wrote, “Omg my baby father @TheRealBSmyth followed me Jesus I love u so much.”

A bunch of them even often asked the singer if he ever considered dating a fan. Sadly, Smyth never replied.

B Smyth Parents

B Smyth was very close to his parents; especially his mother Tracie Freeman-Smith.

After his death, his mom took it to her FB to write a heartbreaking post, “I KNOW THAT THE SOUL NEVER DIES. I WILL SEE YOU AGAIN SON. THIS IS THE HARDEST THING I HAVE EVER BEEN THROUGH. WHAT LESSON AM I SUPPOSED TO LEARN FROM THIS?”

Back in May 2013, Smyth also talked about his mother. “Mom you are my Rock. U keep me grounded and Luv me Unconditionally. #Salute to you for being the Mother & Father,” he wrote.

Guess, Smyth’s dad wasn’t in his life.

B Smyth Songs

B Smyth released multiple hits including Twerkholic, Ride Good, Too Many Reasons, Friends, Creep, Gold Wrappers, and more.

He had over 274K monthly listeners on Spotify in 2022.

As for his popular collaborations, it was with rapper 2 Chainz for his debut single “Leggo,” which peaked at No. 12 on Billboard’s Hot R&B Songs chart; with Future on “Win, Win” and “Gettin’ That”; and with singer LightSkinKeisha on “Ride Good” that generated more than 30 million views on YouTube.

Thanks to his popularity, Smyth’s mech, designed hoodies, were selling fast for $100.

Looking back at his career, Smyth started by posting his covers of popular songs on YouTube, including “Stay” by Rihanna and “Quickie” by Miguel. Thereafter, he was invited to perform at various high-profile talent shows, and soon, he landed a record deal with Motown Records in 2012.

Two years later, he switched record labels from Motown to ByStorm Entertainment and RCA Records.
