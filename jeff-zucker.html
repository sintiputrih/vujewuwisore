Jeff Zucker is one of the important American former media executives.  In addition, Jeff Zucker was the president of CNN Worldwide between January 2013 and February 2022. Early Life

Zucker was born on April 9, 1965. His birthplace is in Homestead, Florida, near Miami. Moreover, Zucker was born into an upper-class Jewish family. As of 2022, Jeff is 57 years old. Likewise, the media executive, Jeff belongs to American nationality. Based on Jeff’s date of birth, his zodiac sign is Aries.

Similarly, Jeff Zucker was born to Mathew Zucker and Arline. His father, Matthew was a cardiologist. On the other hand, his mother, Arline was a professional school teacher. Also, he was a captain of the North Miami Senior High School tennis team. Talking about his educational background, he graduated from North Miami Senior High School in 1982.

For journalism, he took part in Northwestern University’s National High School Institute program. Afterward, he went on to Harvard University. He graduated from Harvard in 1986 with a Bachelor of Arts in American history.

Professional Life and Career

During his initial career, he used to work at NBC by accepting an internship at the 1988 Summer Olympics. In the year 2989, he served as a field producer for Today. Later on, he became its executive producer in 1992. In 2000, Jeff became the president of NBC Entertainment.

Additionally, he became president of NBC’s Entertainment, News & Cable Group in December 2003. In May 2004, he was president of the newly formed NBC Universal Television Group. Since 2007, he served as the president as well as CEO of NBC Universal.

Besides, Jeff has worked with fellow NBC News alum, former Today host Katie Couric. On January 1, 2013, he was assigned as the president of CNN Worldwide. There was an announcement of Zucker being the chairman of WarnerMedia News & Sports’s news and sports division in March 2019. Unfortunately, the National Association of Black Journalists (NABJ) criticized Zucker and CNN for a lack of diversity within the CNN ranks. Ultimately,  he resigned from CNN on February 2, 2022.
