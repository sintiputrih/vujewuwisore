John Ouellette Obituary, Death Cause – It is with profound sadness that we announce the passing of Dundee Police Sgt. John’s journey in service to his country and community began in 1975 when he joined the United States Army. Displaying dedication and discipline, he served with honor until 1978, contributing to the nation’s defense during a crucial period. His military service laid the foundation for a lifelong commitment to protecting and serving others.

Following his military tenure, John pursued a career in law enforcement. He attended the police academy and joined the Monroe Police Department, now known as the Monroe Public Safety Department City of Monroe, MI. Over the course of 29 years, he demonstrated exemplary service, earning the respect and admiration of his colleagues and the community he served. In 2010, John retired from the Monroe Police Department, leaving behind a legacy of dedication, professionalism, and integrity.

Retirement did not mark the end of John’s public service. In 2011, he brought his wealth of experience and passion for policing to the Dundee Police Department. Displaying leadership qualities, he was promoted to the rank of Sergeant in 2015. Throughout his time with the Dundee Police Department, John continued to make a positive impact on the community, earning the trust of those he served and worked alongside.

A dedicated officer, John found joy in his work, particularly during his afternoon shifts, where he continued to make a difference in the lives of Dundee residents. However, in 2021, facing health challenges, John reluctantly took a medical leave, signaling the end of an era in his active duty. Beyond his professional accomplishments, John will be remembered for his kindness, compassion, and the genuine care he showed for his colleagues and the community. His legacy extends beyond the uniform, touching the lives of those who had the privilege of knowing him.
