MLB Contracts

Entry Level Contract

New York Yankees drafted Derek out of high school sixth overall in the first round of the 1992 MLB Draft.

He was immediately signed to the team in a one-year rookie contract worth $800,000 with an average annual salary of the same amount.

Jeter had to extend his stay with the team four more times before making a considerable leap regarding the contract value.

During this time he was binded in one year $109,000 contract in 1995, $130,000 in 1996, $550,000 in 1997 and $750,000 in 1998.

In 1999, he signed a one-year $5 million contract with the Yankees with an average annual salary of the same amount.

Veteran Contract

After the contract expired, the Yankees tossed yet another one-year contract to Derek in 2000, but this time, the value of the contract was doubled to $10 million.

Nevertheless, Jeter’s biggest payday came when the Yankees agreed $189 million ten years contract in 2001.

This was one of the biggest contracts at the time and still is the 24th largest contract in MLB history.

According to the deal, Derek received $16 million as a signing bonus and an average salary of $18.9 million.

In 95, Derek earned a $5 million salary, $10 million in 2000, and by the time the ten-year contract kicked in, he was earning $20 million per season.

Jeter’s peak earnings came in 2010 when he earned a $22.6 million in salary.

After this massive contract expired in 2010, the Yankees tossed another three contracts to Derek, which he signed in December of 2010.

There was no signing bonus with this one, but his average annual salary as per the contract was $17 million.

In 2014, Jeter signed the final extension of his playing career as well as with the Yankees worth $12 million with an average annual salary of the same amount.

Miami Marlins

In 2017, Derek, along with a group of investors, finalized a deal to purchase the Miami Marlins for a whopping $1.2 billion.

Out of the total amount, Jeter’s portion was $25 million, a discounted rate for 4% in the team.

He also served as the CEO of the team for five years.

Unfortunately, the team saw a massive valuation drop to $990 million by 2021.

Derek’s 4% stake was worth $40 million at this point which was better than what he had invested in the first place, but things were not great at the time.

In late February 2022, Jeter’s relationships with the other stakeholders severed, which led him to sell his 4% stake back to his partners at a $1.12 billion valuation.

All in all, Derek ultimately sold his stake in the team for $44.8 million in 2022.
