Who is Rain Pryor?

Rain Pryor is a dynamic speaker, spokesperson, award-winning actress, singer, writer, comedian, and, producer. In 2008, she was awarded Marin Services For Women Founders Award.

Rain Pryor: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Rain Pryor was born on 16 July 1969, in Los Angeles, California. She is 52 years old. Her nationality is American and her ethnicity is mixed(African-American and Ashkenazi Jewish).

She is the daughter of Richard Pryor and Shelley R. Bonus (astronomer/writer). Her maternal grandfather, Herbert Bonis, was Danny Kaye’s manager for 35 years.

Her father was married and divorced 6 times. She has three brothers and two sisters. Rain suffered from drug abuse for many years but got herself clean in 1993.

Rain Pryor has since acted as an addiction counselor and spoke at high schools about her past battles with drugs and alcohol addiction.

Education

Rain Pryor went to the same High School as Angelina Jolie, Michael Klesic, Nicolas Cage, Jonathan Silverman, David Schwimmer, Breckin Meyer, and Richard Dreyfuss.

Rain Pryor graduated from Beverly Hills School in 1987.

Rain Pryor: Professional Life and Career

Rain Pryor went on to star in the ABC series Head of the Class in the role of Theola June ‘T, J,’ Jones. Rain’s role was created from a series of characters. She performed at her audition for the producers.

Pryor starred for several years as Jackie, the lipstick lesbian drug addict on the Showtime series Rude Awakening, and has guest-starred on network television well as The Late Show with Craig Ferguson and The Tavis Smiley Show.

Pryor created and toured in the award-winning show based on her life, Fried Chicken and Latkes, in 2004 and 2005.

In 2012, Rain was named the artistic director of Baltimore’s Strand Theater. Rain Pryor’s work is never done, speaking around the country on the themes of multicultural diversity, family, and Judaism. She brings a unique and touching story to her talks.

In addition, Rain Pryor is an MS Ambassador. Her commitment to fighting for a cure for MS has her hosting and speaking around the world to educate families and friends about MS treatment options and therapies in honor of her father Richard Pryor.
