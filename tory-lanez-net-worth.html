Tory Lanez Net Worth – $5 million

What is Tory Lanez’s net worth?

Introduction

Tory Lanez is a Canadian rapper, songwriter, record producer, and entrepreneur.

He won four Juno Awards and was nominated for a Grammy Award for the hit song “Luv.”

The Origin Story

On July 2nd, 1992, Tory was in Brampton, Ontario, Canada, to his father, Sonstar, and late mother, Luella.

Before relocating to Miami, Florida, he spent a few years of his early life in Montreal, Canada, where his parents wanted to settle permanently.

Following the tragic passing of his mother when Tory was 11 years old, his father became a missionary which required they regularly relocate across America.

Tory and his father would eventually settle in Atlanta, Georgia.

When Tory was 14 years old, his father struggled to deal with his behavioral issues.

He sent him to reside with his cousin in Queens, New York City, hoping they would be better equipped to manage Tory.

Less than two years later, when he was 16 years old, he was sent to Toronto, Ontario, to reside with his grandma.

The living situation didn’t go as planned, so Tory moved downtown alone and needed to learn to be self-sufficient in his teen years.

Tory gravitated toward performing and rapping from a young age.

When he was settled in downtown Toronto, he decided to drop out of tenth grade to perform downtown in busy locations.

Tory began writing and singing his songs to occupy his time when he wasn’t performing and created a mixtape.

T.L 2 T.O.

In 2009, he successfully released  “T.L 2 T.O.,” a 16 track mixtape.

Following this release, he began uploading freestyle and music videos to his YouTube page, which quickly caught the attention of American singer Sean Kingston.

They connected and began working together on future projects.

In 2010, Tory released four consecutive mixtapes titled “Just Landed,” “One Verse One Hearse,” “Playing For Keeps,” and “Mr. 1 Verse Killah.”

By this time, his popularity had ballooned, and his mixtapes performed well with his growing fanbase.

In 2011, Kingston signed Tory to his label – Time is Money Entertainment.

He quickly hit the studio and started working on more mixtapes.

The following year, he released “Mr. Peterson,” “Chixtape,” and “Swavey.”

While Tory was appreciative of his experience with the label and the associations it presented him, he decided to leave as he preferred to be an independent artist. He chose to create his own sound and music and build his brand the way he envisioned it.

Tory was determined to become a world-known artist, so he spent most of his time in his studio when he wasn’t promoting his music.

Between 2012 and 2014, he released three more independent mixtapes, which include “Sincerely Tory,” “Conflicts of My Soul: The 416 Story,” and “Chixtape II.”

@Getty

In 2014, Tory founded the record label, One Umbrella. It originally started as a clothing line and began signing artists in 2018.

In early 2014, he released the song “The Mission,” which was a celebratory release to announce his upcoming tour, the Lost Cause Tour.

He signed to Mad Love Records and Interscope Records following his tour, where he began working on his next album.

On June 26th, 2015, Tory released his debut extended play mixtape titled “Cruel Intentions.”

On July 15th, he released the first song from the album called “B.L.O.W.” The song was a massive hit and ranked in nearly 25 million YouTube views as of 2021.

On December 25th, 2015, Tory released two additional mixtapes titled “Chixtape III” and “The New Toronto.”

On April 1st, 2016, he publicly released that he would be touring in the Level Up Tour with ASAP Ferg, an American rapper.

They collaborated on the song “Line Up the Flex”  to promote the kickstart of their tour. That summer, he performed at New York’s Summer Jam festival and the Pemberton Music Festival in Pemberton, British Columbia.

On May 6th, 2016, Tory released two songs, “For Real” and “Unforgetful” Only two months later, on July 29th, he released the single “Luv.”

It went on to reach No. 96 on the US Billboard Top 100 and No. 96 on the Canadian Hot 100 and peaked at No. 19.

The single was on the charts for 22 weeks, and it was his first single to reach the top 20. Furthermore, the single was nominated for Best R&B Song at the 59th Annual Grammy Awards.

On April 19th, 2016, Tory released another mixtape titled “I Told You.”

The following day, he publicly released his next tour dates for his new album.

He released remixed songs and performed at live concerts for the next two years. On March 2nd, 2018, he released the album “Memories Don’t Die” and “Love Me Now?” on October 26th, 2018.

He went on to tour and promoted both albums.

On November 15th, 2019, Tory released the mixtape’s “Chixtape 5” and “The New Toronto 3” on April 10th, 2020.

Following the release of these albums, he decided to depart from Interscope Records.

On September 25th, 2020, Tory released “Daystar,” his first album since exiting Interscope, and he performed digital events to promote the album.
