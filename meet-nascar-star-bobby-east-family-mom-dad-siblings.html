Meet NASCAR Star Bobby East Dad, Bob East

Bobby East, a race car driver, is the son of Robert “Bob” East.

Bob Sr was the well-known midget, sprint, and silver crown car manufacturer and USAC Hall of Fame inductee Bob East. Moreover, Bob Sr. created the chassis for the Steve Lewis Racing No. 9 that his son raced to victory in the USAC National Midget Series in 2004.

  • Bob East Age

Bob East, who was born in October 1951, is 70 years old.

  • Bob East Job

Bob East is one of the most well-known car builders and team leaders in USAC racing history. Under his leadership, the tenacious Nine Racing team won more than 150 features and 12 USAC National Midget Championships. Bob East has won four Silver Crown championships with Tony Stewart. His vehicles have also won the Silver Crown championships alongside Bobby East, Jason Leffler, Mike Bliss, and Ryan Newman.

Two of Bob East’s midgets, Josh Wise and Ralph Potter, won titles in the early 1990s and 2005, respectively. More recently, in a Beast, Darren Hagen won the USAC National Midget Series Pavement Championships three times in a row from 2010 to 2012.

In 1988, Bob founded B. East Enterprises and introduced what is perhaps the best chassis racing on USAC tracks today. Since 1991, his Beast chassis have helped the Nine Racing team win every Silver Crown National Championship and the National USAC Midget Championships. East began his career as a driver in the West Coast sprint car circuit, where he won both CRA and USAC events.

The current address for East Enterprises in Pittsboro, Indiana is 3811 E CR 1000 N.
